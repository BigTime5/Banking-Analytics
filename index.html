Problem Statement

Project Title: Comprehensive Analytics for Banking Transaction Data to Enhance Risk Management, Customer Insights, and Operational Efficiency

Core Problem:
The bank currently lacks a holistic, data-driven understanding of customer transaction behaviors, resulting in potential blind spots in fraud detection, customer retention, channel optimization, and risk mitigation across merchants, geographies, and transaction patterns. With a dataset containing 2,512 transactions from 495 unique customers (total volume ~$747,556), there is a significant opportunity to extract actionable intelligence through advanced analytics.

Intent of the Project:
To conduct end-to-end exploratory, predictive, and prescriptive analytics on banking transaction data, delivering insights across eight key domains: fraud detection, customer segmentation, transaction behavior prediction, churn risk assessment, geographical patterns, channel preferences, login & transaction duration analysis, and merchant risk profiling. The ultimate goal is to transform raw transactional data into a robust intelligence framework that enables proactive, evidence-based decision-making.

Key Business Questions Addressed

  • How can we accurately detect and prioritize potential fraudulent transactions to reduce financial losses?
  • Who are our distinct customer segments, and how do their spending, frequency, channel preferences, and lifetime value differ?
  • What temporal and behavioral patterns exist in transactions, and can we reliably predict future activity?
  • Which customers are at high risk of churning, and what early warning signals should we monitor?
  • How do transaction volumes, risks, and customer demographics vary across geographic locations?
  • Which channels (ATM, Online, Branch) are preferred by different demographics, and what are the security and efficiency implications?
  • What factors influence transaction duration and login attempts, and how do these relate to potential security risks?
  • Which merchants exhibit high-risk patterns based on transaction variability and login behavior?
